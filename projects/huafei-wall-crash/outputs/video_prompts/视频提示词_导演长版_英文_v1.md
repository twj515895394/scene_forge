## global_execution_preamble

Treat the provided 5 storyboard packs as **sequential visual keyframe references**, not loose inspiration. Strictly follow the storyboard's beat progression, camera rhythm, composition logic, action choreography, spatial relationships, and emotional progression to drive frame generation.

Between adjacent storyboard key poses, expand into smooth, continuous, cinematic animated motion — do not produce frame-by-frame tableaux. Every action unit follows: anticipation → force → reaction → release. Preserve the action direction and performance timing already established in the storyboard.

**Cinematic in-between motion expansion**: Adjacent key poses in the storyboard represent critical action waypoints. Between them, generate fluid physical transitions — fabric continues to move after a character stops, hair settles after a turn, particles dissipate after impact. Do not snap from pose to pose.

**Do not render** storyboard borders, arrows, shot numbers, panel dividers, UI annotations, subtitles, or any storyboard artifacts. The storyboard is a visual roadmap, not a frame template — preserve its spatial logic and action relationships, but present them as complete cinematic continuous shots.

## project_level_global_rules

**Main Scene Space Lock**: The cold palace / studio backlot set runs through the entire piece. Foam pillar fixed at frame left (Zone B left). Rear wall / LED screen fixed at frame center-back. Zones A/B/C/D positions remain constant throughout. Rear wall never drifts to the side.

**Character Reappearance Lock**: Shilan maintains the same character design every time she appears — Qing dynasty consort, rounded oval face, almond eyes with willow-leaf brows, plain white cotton-linen qizhuang robe, simplified liangbatou hairstyle. Facial proportions, body silhouette, and costume details must remain consistent across all shots — no drift, no mutation.

**No Character Duplication**: A single character never appears twice in the same frame. No unmotivated duplication, multiplication, or substitution within a continuous scene.

**Readability Priority** (subject-agnostic, applies to all shots):
- Motion clarity: every action's force direction, movement path, and recovery pose must be immediately legible
- Silhouette recognition: Shilan's qizhuang outline and liangbatou side profile remain clearly identifiable at any shot scale
- Spatial stability: the foam pillar, rear wall, candlelight — all spatial anchors must not drift across shot changes
- Performance timing accuracy: expression transition rhythms strictly follow storyboard timecodes

**Style Lock (classic_studio_wuxia)**:
- Studio backlot aesthetic throughout: the set is a soundstage build — preserve its "stage-bound" quality rather than pursuing photoreal location realism
- Establishing tableau: key actions receive a brief "presentation" pause before execution (the classic wuxia "entrance and stance" order)
- Complete action display: action chains (sprint → impact → rebound → sit) are never chopped into quick-cut fragments
- Entrance/exit order: Shilan's movements have clear spatial positioning and directional clarity

**Lighting Lock**: Studio-style rim light with character/zone priority. Warm candlelight key (~2700K) from low angle on Shilan's face. Cool moonlight fill (~5600K) diffused from above through high window. Light ratio maintained at approximately 3:1. No modern naturalistic flat lighting. Seg7-8: golden hour sunset backlight (3200→2500K gradient), long shadows (3m→8m). Seg8 H04 on: soundstage cool white LED (5600K) + warm softbox (3200K) vs. cold palace warm candles (2700K) — the warm/cool world boundary.

**Negative Constraints**:
- Do not apply modern fast-cut action film logic
- Do not modernize or colloquialize character performance style
- Do not rely solely on old-film filter aesthetics without blocking and stance discipline
- All impact effects maintain a non-realistic, harm-free, "performance can continue" presentation
- Camera language serves only narrative, emotion, action readability, and visual information
- Do not reference or imitate any specific film's shots — describe abstract shot structures only
- LED screen penetration is a flexible panel separation design — no shattered glass, no sharp edges, no sparks
